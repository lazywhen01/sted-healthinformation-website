What is Circulatory System?

April 2024 @educ

The Circulatory System transports blood from the heart to the lungs for oxygenation, then distributes oxygen-rich blood throughout the body via arteries. Veins return oxygen-depleted blood to the heart, maintaining the circulation cycle essential for the health of organs, muscles, and tissues. The circulatory system comprises your heart and blood vessels. Its primary role is to deliver oxygen, nutrients, and hormones to muscles, tissues, and organs across your body.

Additionally, it aids in eliminating waste from cells and organs, facilitating their disposal by the body. The circulatory system can also be referred to as your cardiovascular system. Cardio means heart, and vascular refers to blood vessels.

Organ/Component Function
Heart A muscular organ that pumps blood throughout your body.
Blood Vessels Distribute blood around the body.
Arteries Carry blood from the heart to capillaries.
Capillaries Permit diffusion between blood and interstitial fluid.
Veins Return blood from capillaries to the heart.
Blood Transports oxygen, carbon dioxide, and blood cells; delivers nutrients and hormones; removes waste products; assists in temperature regulation and defense against disease.

What are the circulatory system circuits?

1. Pulmonary circuit transports deoxygenated blood from the heart to the lungs, while oxygenated blood is returned to the heart via the pulmonary veins.

2. Systemic circuit oxygenated blood containing nutrients and hormones is pumped from the heart to all parts of the body. As the body consumes oxygen, nutrients, and hormones, waste products are collected in the veins.

3. Coronary Circuit which pertains to the arteries of the heart, supplies oxygenated blood to the cardiac muscle. Subsequently, it transports oxygen-depleted blood back to the right atrium of the heart, from where it is sent to the lungs for oxygenation.
