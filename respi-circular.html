Respiratory System and Circulatory System

April 2024 @educ

The cardiovascular system, or circulatory system, circulates blood throughout your body, collaborating with your respiratory system to deliver oxygen-rich blood to your cells. Breathing commences as you inhale air through your nose or mouth, which then travels down your throat and enters the windpipe, known as the trachea, which branches into airways called bronchial tubes.

For your lungs to perform their best, these airways need to be open. They should be free from inflammation or swelling and extra mucus. As the bronchial tubes traverse your lungs, they branch into smaller airways known as bronchioles, which terminate in small, balloon-like structures called alveoli. Your body contains approximately 600 million alveoli. These alveoli are encircled by a network of minute blood vessels called capillaries, where oxygen from inhaled air diffuses into your bloodstream.

After absorbing oxygen, blood goes to your heart. Your heart then pumps it through your body to the cells of your tissues and organs. As the cells use the oxygen, they make carbon dioxide that goes into your blood. Your blood then carries the carbon dioxide back to your lungs, where it is removed from your body when you exhale.
