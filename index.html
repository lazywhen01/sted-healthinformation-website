Circulatory System

American Physiological Society

April 2024 @educ

The Circulatory System transports blood from the heart to the lungs for oxygenation, then distributes oxygen-rich blood throughout the body via arteries. Veins return oxygen-depleted blood to the heart, maintaining the circulation cycle essential for the health of organs, muscles, and tissues. The circulatory system comprises your heart and blood vessels. Its primary role is to deliver oxygen, nutrients, and hormones to muscles, tissues, and organs across your body. Additionally, it aids in eliminating waste from cells and organs, facilitating their disposal by the body. The circulatory system can also be referred to as your cardiovascular system. Cardio means heart, and vascular refers to blood vessels.

Respiratory System

American Physiological Society

April 2024 @educ

The respiratory system is the biological system in organisms that enables breathing and exchange of gases between the body and the environment. It includes several organs and structures involved in the process of respiration, which is essential for supplying oxygen to the body's cells and removing carbon dioxide, a waste product of cellular metabolism.
